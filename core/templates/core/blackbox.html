What is the Mystery Black Box Project?

In our New Media and Technology class, we examined the Mystery Black Box, a large black case with a funnel at the top and a pipe coming out the bottom. We do not know what is inside the mystery box, but we did know that something inside the box made it so that if we poured the same amount of water in to the box multiple times, the amount of water that came out was different every time. Our objective was to figure out what makes the box do this.

What do I think is in the mystery black box?

I believe that the mystery black box has tubing going in different directions, leading to a bucket with a siphon at its top side.

Our experiment

To collect data, we poured water into the funnel and observed how much water came out. We notice that when we pour a specific amount of water, in this case 1000 mL was used, we get various outputs in terms of the amount of water that comes out of the black box. We found that sometimes more than 1000 mL would come out, and sometimes it would be less. There were times when 0 mL of water came out of the box as well.

How we designed our model

Our model of the black box has to explain the variation in the amount of water that comes out. One of the concepts my team included to explain this was the fact that the tube is in a rather strange shape. This means that when there isn’t a direct flow of water through the tube, the water could be held there until we do pour the water in.

Our group also included a bucket, which collects the water from the tubing, with a hole at the top side. The shape of the tubing would explain the reason why we have more water than the amount we pour in at times, because the leftover water in the pipes would flow along with the water we pour, making it seem like more water has appeared. Because of the way we designed the bucket and tubing, the water wouldn’t come out of the bucket until it was completely full, the amount of which is something that can change depending on the amount of times we pour water into the bucket. Additionally, this change is random, which explains how we get random amounts of water coming out of the black box each time.

The data of our model was generally random, similar to that of the black box. I think that if a different claim was correct, the data of the model resulting from that claim would have more variation. While our data did have variation, it only decreased from the amount we poured in. This is probably a result of the excessive leakage of our model. I think that if alternative design was used, the data would have been similar to the black box and include outputs that were both increases and decreases from our inputs.
